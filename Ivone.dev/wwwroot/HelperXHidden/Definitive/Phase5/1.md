## What To Learn
The data model centers around schools, pupils, assessments, subjects, and terms. These entities form the backbone of reporting.

## Why It Exists
All reports and calculations depend on consistent relationships between these entities.

## Where It Lives In The Codebase
- Entity definitions: `sonar-api/sonar-data/Entity/*`
- Database context: `sonar-api/sonar-data/SonarDbContext.cs`

## Core Entity Groups
1. **School And Configuration**
   - School, terms, academic years, feature entitlements.
2. **Pupils**
   - Pupil records, group membership, characteristics.
3. **Subjects**
   - Subjects linked to assessments and reports.
4. **Assessments**
   - Summative, formative, statutory, test scores.
5. **Users And Roles**
   - User profiles, roles, permissions.

## Relationships (High-Level)
- A school has many pupils, subjects, and terms.
- A pupil has many assessments and observations.
- Assessments link pupil + subject + assessment point.

## Questions And Answers
- **Which entities are write-heavy vs read-heavy?**
  - Write-heavy: assessments, observations.
  - Read-heavy: report queries over assessments, subjects, and pupils.

## Additional Questions You Should Be Able To Answer
- Where are assessment codes stored?
  - Summative and ARE codes are defined by entities in the summative and age-related expectation tables.
